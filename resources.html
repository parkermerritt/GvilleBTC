Helpful Links and Videos

While Bitcoin is known to have a pretty steep learning curve, there are many online resources that new users might find helpful. Listed below are a few of our favorites.

What is Bitcoin?

A Talk From Our Founder

The founder of the Gainesville Bitcoin community, Brandon, shares some of his thoughts about the history and use cases of Bitcoin at a local meetup.

Lopp's Resources

Bitcoin Engineer's Curated Picks

BitGo Engineer Jameson Lopp has spent years curating a massive open-source list of tutortials, videos, and resources.

Internet of Money

Andreas Explains Bitcoin's Revolution

Bitcoin evangelist Andreas Antonopoulos discusses how open blockchains will soon revolutionize finance, governments, the internet, and the world as we know it.

Bitcoin White Paper

The Birth of Blockchain

On October 31, 2008, an anonymous programmer by the name of "Satoshi Nakamoto" published the first proposal for Bitcoin on a cryptography mailing list. While a few aspects of the paper may seem abstract to a non-technical reader, the document is still widely considered mandatory reading for all Bitcoin beginners.
